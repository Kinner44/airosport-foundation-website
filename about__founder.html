Meet the Founder

He built a sport for a son who couldn't play the ones we had.

The moment.

Mike Kinner watched other kids play the games every kid is supposed to get to play — and watched his son Samuel, who is medically complex, never get the chance. Somewhere in there a dad made a decision: the problem wasn't Samuel. It was the games.

The build.

So he spent eight years inventing a target-based sport with one non-negotiable rule: anyone can play, and anyone can win — same field, same shot, same shot at glory.

The proof.

Companies used it to build trust between coworkers. Families played it in driveways. But the people who lit up hardest were the ones sports had never made room for.

The turn.

That's when "invent a sport" became "start a foundation" — so a peak moment never depends on whether a family can afford one.

"I'm not a nonprofit guy. I'm a dad who built a game. This is just where it led." — Mike Kinner, Founder
